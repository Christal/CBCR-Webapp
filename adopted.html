Recent Adoptions

Ava

Ava is a beautiful, petite, split faced Border Collie with a charming under-bite that makes her look as if she's smiling all the time and makes everyone she meets smile too. She came to us from a high-kill shelter in NC where she was admitted as a stray just days before Christmas. It was discovered that she was microchipped, but when the shelter contacted the owner, they were told that she had been given away to someone else. She was not reclaimed. A team of volunteers from various rescue organizations helped us spring Ava from the shelter in time for Christmas. She has won over the hearts of every person she has met along the way with her gentle, sweet and loving personality.

When found, Ava was covered in fleas and she has some dermatitis that is likely related to that, but that is healing nicely now that she is free of fleas, is clean, and has a healthy diet. She tolerates an occasional bath with poise. Ava is extremely well behaved, is completely housebroken, and gets along with her foster family's exuberant and overly playful Labrador Retriever mix female. Mostly the lab wants to play and Ava wants to herd. Ava obliges her in chase, but with a Border Collie stare, stature and bossiness that says "This is not chase. I'm herding you, Lab." So, Ava appears to have the Border Collie genes, but she also has a very nice "off switch" and loves to snuggle on the couch equally as much or maybe even more than being in action. I have not tested her with children, but she is so gentle and loving with people that she should be fine with older children.

Ava appears to have been someone's pet at one time, as she is confident, polite, and comfortable inside the house and out. She is so smart, loyal and loving. She has already learned all basic commands like sit, down, stay, come, shake, and touch. At first she was quite “velcro” with her foster mom but she has relaxed and doesn't follow me everywhere anymore, preferring now to simply check to see where I am, and then go back to whatever she was doing (usually to nap on the couch!). Ava has been a perfect dog in the house. She does not chew on inappropriate objects, counter surf, bark much, guard food or toys, or try to escape the fence or jump the baby gate. She is very focused on her people and wants very much to please. We are especially working on walking on leash and learning not to jump on people when she is excited to see you. She can be exuberant and strong to greet people and dogs when walking on leash and sometimes reactive to loud vehicles in close proximity, but with work and practice she is greatly improving.

She is being crated at night and sleeps well and quietly until her family awakes. Ava is still a little nervous in the car but rides wonderfully in the back seat. The car nervousness should pass as she discovers that car rides equal going to fun places. On Ava's hikes she is very friendly with all adults, kids, and dogs along the way and makes lots of new friends. She also has showed no fear of the loud, repeated gunshots heard from across the valley while hiking and at home, which bodes well that she is not thunder or loud noise phobic.

Ava has shown an unhealthy interest in the resident cat, and they are being kept separated at this time. A home without cats is recommended.

I believe Ava would make a wonderful therapy dog due to her sweet and loving demeanor and because every single person who sees her cute little underbite and face can't help but to crack a huge smile and giggle. I think she would also enjoy agility and other sports. And although not tested, she seems to have some good herding instinct. Mostly, she is also a professional snuggler and accomplished couch potato and is content with a moderate level of activity. She is definitely a medium-energy pup. Her favorite thing to do is to gaze lovingly into your eyes and snuggle against you to show her immense gratitude for rescuing her. She is appreciative and deserving of a special, loving life and she will make someone a wonderful companion.

Kimbo

Kimbo is a lover! He loves to lay on the couch cuddling his people. His favorite thing is scooting right up, putting his head on a pillow and falling asleep with his paw in my hand. If he can't be snuggling he is also perfect in his crate or chewing a bone on the floor.

This guy would make an amazing hiking buddy. He has enough get up and go to keep up with an active lifestyle, but is not hyper. The fun loving, high energy dog out on the trail almost seems like a different dog from the sweet, calm, cuddly boy inside. The occasional quiet or rainy day doesn't bother him, either; a little bit of rolling around with his squeaky toy and he's back to cuddling.

Kimbo loves other dogs. He plays wonderfully with males and females of all sizes, and is respectful if someone doesn't want to play. He is currently living with three cats and is doing very well. If a cat swats at him he walks away. He usually doesn't give into chasing them, but if he does all he wants is to ask them to play with a bow!

Kimbo is a happy, confident dog who enjoys meeting new people and dogs. He responds quickly to manners training, comes when called and has learned to sit for what he wants. Such a smart dog who enjoys figuring out what his people want from him. Kimbo still needs work with pulling on leash- but that's the only negative thing I can think of about this guy! He's a great boy who would be a perfect addition to an active home with other dogs.

While this condition has a good prognosis for his future well-being as an active dog, it needs to be addressed and we are currently looking into the best treatment option. We will update his bio once we know more, but for the time being he is not available for adoption.

TJ is a lot of fun, a super sweet and very active boy! He adores all people, his favorite thing on walks is to meet children in the neighborhood, he loves them and has to be kept away from strollers to make sure he doesn't climb in and lick the little one's face! He has met cats and has been very interested but very polite, he followed the cat around but walked away as soon as the cat turned and gave him a stern look. He gets along with any dog, being very playful and just submissive enough not to get in trouble. He absolutely loves to chase, tug and wrestle with other dogs, having a playmate at home would be an excellent way to burn off some of his energy! He was the star at doggie daycare the first time he went with his sweet nature.

TJ hadn't been taught much of anything until he came into foster care but is catching up quickly, he walks well on a leash, can sit and lay down and already has very good recall, actually he is so happy to be called and get a good cuddle that he can't come running fast enough! He is very attentive and eager to do the right thing. He would make a fantastic sports dog, agility, flyball, anything really. TJ loves water and jumps right in without fear. His herding potential has not been tested at this point. TJ needs an active home, this boy would love to play all day long! He still has a tendency to chew on things when bored but can be redirected to appropriate chew toys and is used to being in a crate if needed. Having a fenced in yard would be ideal as he is quite good at entertaining himself racing around in big circles with anything he can grab in his mouth. Even though he has yet to discover the world he is game for anything, hops in the car and just deals with any new experience cautiously but courageously, always keeping an eye on his foster mom and foster sibling for guidance. This boy is an expert snuggler, if he could he would sit on his foster mom's lap at all times and his favorite way of taking a nap is with both his arms wrapped around his foster mom's leg! He will not be adopted to a home with electric fencing or any other shock type training system.

Penny

Penny knows how to use a dog door and is very close to being potty trained. Crossing my fingers - she has not had an accident in the house in the past two days. Over the past week she has only had a few accidents.

Penny is very smart, she know several commands so far – she will come to her name, knows sit, stay and go potty outside.

Penny loves to eat, and does not seem to have food issues.

She loves to play ball and play with other dogs, she will play tug-a-war with our Border Collie over any furry toy and constantly wrestles with him.

Penny walks well on a leash but does a zig-zag pattern when she walks. If you keep her on a short leash she will stay to one side.

She is a lap dog and loves to have her belly rubbed/scratched. She has the softest belly fur I have ever felt, like velvet. She melts like butter in your arms.

Penny sleeps on a pillow beside our bed or where ever she decides to plop down.

Stokie

Stokie is her own homemade miracle of strength and determination. She came to CBCR from a small high-kill shelter in central NC where she was very near death; not only from being hours away from euthanization, but also from the very poor health condition she was in. She had pneumonia, a fever, she was shaking uncontrollably, and she refused to go outside. She was a terribly skinny, a tangled rug of mats, and had very long and painful toenails.

Assessing all of her conditions required an overnight stay at the vet and many subsequent visits. It was determined that she had been hit by a car at some point, and had a small puncture in her lung. She made a horrendous sound when she would breathe or drink water. She held one of her back legs up high and would not use it. Even in her foster home, she was afraid to go outside at all. She ate very little. During the first few weeks in her foster home, Stokie's foster mom had to check to make sure Stokie was still breathing as she mostly laid there and didn't move.

After two rounds of antibiotics (with lots of pill pockets, peanut butter, cream cheese and Vienna sausages) Stokie miraculously began to show life again. She started eating a high calorie canned food diet from the veterinarians for being underweight. She started going outside with company, then got more secure every day. The more her health and appetite improved, the better she felt and looked. One day at a time, Stokie was coming back to life.

Stokie is now a different dog. She is a beautiful, senior, likely purebred Border Collie who is quickly learning to enjoy her new life and leave her past neglect and abandonment by the wayside. She and her foster mom move forward every day and everyone is amazed at the very fast pace that Stokie is improving. She now goes out by herself into the fenced yard, no leash required. She is no longer afraid of the wind or a passing plane overhead. She trots her three good legs around in the yard and waits at the door. She plays vigorously on the carpet with chew toys like a cat.

She is definitely a senior gal and likes to sleep a lot due to her age. She lives harmoniously in a house with other dogs and has met cats and ignored them. She may have once lived with cats as she seems to like them. She has a clean bill of health, with the exception of her back leg, which she sometimes uses and sometimes doesn't. After examination by her veterinarian and an orthopedic specialist, the diagnosis is that Stokie has an old torn left quadricep muscle and the leg has atrophied over time. This does not appear to be painful for Stokie and she gets around well, usually on three or three and half legs. At the recommendation of the vet, she has had an MRI, to rule out any other injuries that could be causing her leg issues, and there are None, No Nerve Damage, no pinched Discs and she is Tumor-free, which is rare, in a senior dog. Her orthopedic veterinarian has said that she will likely be fine to live out her days as a three-legged dog as she certainly seems to have a high quality of life at this time.

Given all of her hard times, Stokie is a beautiful and graceful Border Collie and an amazing example of perseverance. She will make an appreciative and loving companion for a less active family.

Lance

Lance is a gentle, sweet, two year old Border Collie male who was found as a stray in the Climax NC area. He loves everyone (cats, not so much...) including all humans and well-mannered dogs and he gets along beautifully with his foster family, their two dogs and visitors to the home. He is eager to greet humans and canines we meet on walks. He seems to prefer the company of men, though is affectionate with everyone. Lance has been remarkably gentle and tolerant of the foster family's three active toddler grandchildren. He loves to roam around in the backyard, go for walks, and play with toys. He's happy to be crated at night (not necessary here, but we do it in case this will be the routine in his new home) and has excellent house manners, especially considering he was probably an outdoor dog in his former life. His house training is excellent. Lance does quite well on a leash, except occasionally when distracted by critters. His one bad habit is lunging at passing cars if they are exceptionally noisy or fast moving and at close range. We are working on this with about a 50% improvement so far. He is calm indoors and more energetic outside.

Lance is generally healthy (low positive for heartworm with treatment scheduled in a few days), up to date on vaccines and neutered. Following his heartworm treatment his exercise will be restricted for five weeks. After his recovery period, he will very likely be an excellent candidate for agility or herding or would make an excellent buddy for jogging or hiking. His energy level, given his age and breed, is medium - certainly not hyper, but not a couch potato either. He is smart and attentive and loves a good challenge. It would be fun to work with him in obedience, agility, etc.! He'd also make a good companion, but like most Border Collies, will need some daily activity - at least brisk walks, daily games of fetch or visits to the dog park.

This boy is exceptionally beautiful and loving and would make a wonderful family member!
